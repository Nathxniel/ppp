│       └── log
this is the journal of the project. it was initially in the 'misc' (now '.p') folder of the project due to its informality, but i decided to move it here because i think the information from it could prove to be useful.

as aforementioned, part of this project is to explore the potential usage of substances on the effect of life strategising and so with each log entry i thought of a yardstick system of tracking drug usage: the 'rel' system.

to demonstrate by example:
'rel 100 alcohol' means 'i have consumed 100% of the most alcohol i have ever drank previously till now at the time of writing this'
the same applies to all other substances made

(to indicate me consuming more than 100% of any given substance, i denoted it with 'new record!')

in the future i will attempt to record substance intake more formally, as dosages but may come back to the system in the log.

a step through the incremental logical thought continuation cycle is tantamount to the logical inference steps you go through when thinking about any topic. 

for example, lets model the mind of a rat who only knows mathematical concepts (topic: maths), concepts of cheese (topic: cheese), and concepts of elephants (topic: elephants). input information will be noise, where noise is defined as some permutation of: sounds, smells, visuals; i.e. input sensory information. the rat will then process this information according to some rules and the idea will express itself within the rules of maths or cheese. two examples: 1) imagine the input information is another rat communicating the idea "there is no feta" to a rat through rat-language. the rat will process this and, provided the rat understands rat-language, form a _thought_ which is about the topic of cheese and maths, but not about elephants. an inference step would then be the new _thought_ "is there no gouda"? to which the output information may be the rat saying back "is there any gouda though?"

2) imagine the input information of some other noise, perhaps the rat looking at an image of feta. the rat will similarly process and form a thought, this time only about cheese and not necessarily about maths or elephants, and the inference step would perhaps be a new _thought_ about feta and the output may be a mental image of feta

you go through the incremental logical continuation cycle at
a certain speed, s.

s increases with certain psychoactive drugs
s decreases with certain depressant drugs

you can see why this can be helpful in some situations and harmful in others; its helpful for s to be higher when learning a skill. the process of learning has to be (done in part) in a "non-test" environment however.

imagine the skill of learning how to play tennis. part of this skill can be 'learned on the job', i.e. by playing tennis matches, as part of the (in this case practical; muscle memory of hitting the ball; comfortability on the court) can be learned whilst also testing, but playing tennis matches is also a test environment because you are testing against the opponent (the tennis opponent). learning in a non-test environment would be watching tennis games, or reading tennis strategy from a book.

a purely test environment then would be a case where a tennis match is being played, and nothing is being learned. perhaps an example of this is when playing a much weaker opponent. i am conjecturing that some part of the learning process can be hindering when testing, for example if you are too busy "thinking" when playing a tennis match you may lose. this is my reasoning behind the theory that in a pure test environment lowering s may be benefitial. this is something i must investigate further going into the new generation of the project.

drugs may have long-term lasting effects on the information processing barrier, which is may obfuscate novel input information into neutral input information and henceforth reduce the rate of learning. this is something i must investigate further going into the new generation of the project.
